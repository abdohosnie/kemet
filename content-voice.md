# Content Voice Guide
## How to write for KEMET

---

## The Standard

A reader opens a pharaoh profile and finishes the page without realizing twenty minutes have
passed. That is the only benchmark that matters. If the writing achieves that, it's working.
If it doesn't, something is wrong — and usually the problem is one of the patterns listed below.

---

## The Voice

**Vivid. Direct. Grounded in consequence.**

KEMET's editorial voice sits between a longform magazine feature and a museum catalogue entry.
It is not an encyclopedia. It is not a lecture. It is a story told with precision.

- Sentences are short or deliberately long — never medium and meandering
- Every paragraph earns its place. If it could be cut without losing anything, cut it.
- The reader is intelligent. Do not explain what is obvious; illuminate what is not.
- Time is always present. We are always aware of how long ago this was, and that it mattered.

---

## Rules

### Lead with the human, not the date

> ✗ **Wrong:** "Thutmose III was an Egyptian pharaoh who reigned from 1479 to 1425 BCE."

> ✓ **Right:** "He spent the first twenty-two years of his reign in someone else's shadow. Hatshepsut, his stepmother, held the power while he waited. When he finally ruled alone, he did not rest. He marched."

---

### Ground the reader in time — without being mechanical

Every profile should make the reader feel the distance of history without belaboring it.

> ✓ "He built this temple twelve hundred years before the Parthenon was laid. Three hundred years before Homer was born."

Do this once, naturally. Not as a formula in every article.

---

### Use concrete numbers, not vague scale

> ✗ "He commanded a vast army."
> ✓ "He marched with four divisions — Amun, Ra, Ptah, and Set — a force of roughly twenty thousand men."

> ✗ "His reign was very long."
> ✓ "He ruled for sixty-four years. Nearly every Egyptian alive when he died had been born under his name."

---

### End sections with consequence

Every section should close on what changed, what survived, or what it cost.

> ✗ "He built the Great Pyramid at Giza, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World."
> ✓ "The pyramid stood. His name is carved inside it in a worker's graffiti — misspelled, rushed,
> and more human than any royal inscription. He has been dead for four and a half thousand years.
> The pyramid has not moved."

---

### Banned words and phrases

Never use these. Find a more precise word.

| Banned | Why |
|---|---|
| *renowned* | Empty. Everything here is renowned. |
| *iconic* | Meaningless in historical writing. |
| *legendary* | Same problem. Earn the description. |
| *fascinating* | You are telling the reader how to feel. Show them instead. |
| *interesting* | Same. |
| *arguably* | Commit or don't. |
| *it is worth noting* | Just note it. |
| *throughout history* | Vague. Which part of history? |
| *played a key role* | In what? How? Be specific. |
| *one of the most* | Usually a sign the sentence needs to be rewritten. |

## Structure of a Pharaoh Biography

Each biography is divided into named subsections. Below are the standard sections and
what belongs in each. Not every pharaoh needs all sections — tailor to what actually happened.

### 1. The Ascent
How did they come to power? What was the world they inherited?
Was it stable, fractured, inherited, seized? Begin with a scene.

### 2. The Ruler
How did they govern? What defined their reign in practice?
Military, administrative, religious, architectural — follow the evidence, not a formula.

### 3. The Builder (if applicable)
What did they construct, commission, or restore?
Name the monuments. Place them. Describe them briefly. Give them weight.

### 4. The War (if applicable)
For pharaohs whose reigns included significant military campaigns.
Name the battles, the enemies, the stakes.

### 5. The Faith
How did religion shape their reign?
Every pharaoh was both a political ruler and a divine figure — this tension is always present.

### 6. The End
How did their reign end? Death, deposition, succession?
What state did they leave the country in?

### 7. The Legacy
What came after? What do we have because they existed?
This is the closing — make it land.

## Length Guidelines

| Content Type | Target Length |
|---|---|
| Era overview | 3–5 sentences |
| Dynasty summary | 4–6 sentences |
| Pharaoh biography (full) | 600–1,200 words |
| Pharaoh biography (minor ruler) | 200–400 words |
| Artifact description | 2–4 sentences |
| Monument description | 3–5 sentences |
| Pull quote (highlighted) | 1 sentence, maximum 20 words |

---

## A Note on Spelling and Dates

- Use **BCE** and **CE**, not BC/AD
- Use **"circa"** or **"c."** for uncertain dates — do not present estimates as fact
- Pharaoh name spellings follow the Oxford History of Ancient Egypt as the primary reference
- Where multiple transliterations exist, use the most widely recognized (e.g., *Ramesses* not *Ramses* not *Ramesses*)
- Ancient Egyptian words and titles should be italicized on first use: *netjer* (god), *per-aa* (pharaoh)

---

## Final Check Before Submitting Content

Read your draft aloud. If you stumble on a sentence, it needs to be rewritten.
If a paragraph feels like a Wikipedia summary, it needs to be rewritten.
If nothing in the section would make someone want to keep reading, it needs to be rewritten.

That's the whole checklist.
